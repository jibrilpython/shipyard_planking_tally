1. DESIGN PHILOSOPHY
Visual: “The Shipwright’s Ledger” — Inspired by 19th-century maritime blueprints and heavy timber construction. 
The UI uses a "structural joinery" approach: clean, interlocking Bento-box panels that mimic the way planks 
are fitted to a frame. Instead of thin lines, use solid, "load-bearing" borders and deep, oceanic depth.

Mood: Timeless, nautical, artisanal, and incredibly stable.

Important: No "tech-startup" gradients or playful animations. Every interaction should feel weighty and 
deliberate. Avoid thin, spindly fonts—opt for something with a bit of "iron" in its weight to reflect 
the hand-forged nature of the tools.

2. COLORS
#0F1216 — Main background (Deep Atlantic / Cold Forge black)
#E4E7EB — Primary text & high contrast elements (Salted canvas / Weathered paper)
#1B1E24 — Secondary panels / input fields (Dark Oak / Wet hull timber)
#828D99 — Secondary text (Mist grey / Oxidized iron)
#2A5B8A — Primary accent (Deep Mariner Blue — steady and traditional)
#2D333B — Outline/Stroke (Cast iron / Bulkhead grey)
#D14343 — Critical errors only (Signal buoy red)

Important: Avoid glossy gradients. Keep the UI matte and high-contrast. Do not add your own colors; 
strictly use the palette provided above to maintain the maritime aesthetic.
